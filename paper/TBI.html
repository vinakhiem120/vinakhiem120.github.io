viTBI-BERT: A Vietnamese Language Model for TBI Prediction

Authors: Doan Duc Khiem, Thanh Hai Tran, Trung-Kien Tran, Thi-Lan Le, Hai Vu, Huu-Khanh Nguyen, Van Mao Can, Thanh Bac Nguyen

Affiliations: Hanoi University of Science and Technology, Military Institute of Science and Technology, Military Hospital 103, Vietnam Military Medical University

Abstract

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is transforming the prediction and classification of Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI). Our research introduces viTBI-BERT, a novel Vietnamese language model based on ViHealthBERT, designed to classify TBI severity from medical text reports.

The model is trained using a self-collected dataset from 503 patients at Military Hospital 103, Hanoi. We apply text preprocessing, data augmentation, and deep learning techniques to enhance classification accuracy. The model achieves 71% sensitivity in classifying four severity levels: mild, moderate, severe, and critical.

Introduction

Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI) is a leading cause of disability worldwide. Traditional diagnostic approaches rely on CT scans, MRIs, and clinical assessment, which can be subjective and resource-intensive. AI-based models provide an alternative approach by analyzing textual medical notes, enabling more efficient and automated TBI classification.
